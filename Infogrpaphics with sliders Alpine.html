The 4-Phase NBP Lesson Flow

This 60-90 minute lesson is structured like a sales cycle, moving students from disinterest to active creation by focusing entirely on their intrinsic motivations.

The Impact: Measuring Success

Success isn't measured by a final exam, but by observing the process and the student's journey. The assessment is qualitative, focusing on engagement, creation, and reflection.

  • üëÄ

    Observation

    Did students show curiosity and engage willingly? Did they experiment without fear of failure?

  • üß©

    Product

    Did they create a simple animation that aligns with a basic need, demonstrating understanding?

  • ü§î

    Reflection

    Can students connect their creation back to their original idea? Are they curious about what's next?

The Iterative Loop: Reflect, Grow, Spark New Needs (Phase 4)

Phase 4 is about consolidating learning, celebrating achievements, and crucially, using reflection to identify new curiosities and challenges. This naturally sparks the next cycle of learning within the Needs-Based Pedagogy framework.
